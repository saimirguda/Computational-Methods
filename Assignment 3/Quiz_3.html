What is true about densities and distributions?

a.
The cumulative distribution at y is the density integrated up to y.
b.
The cumulative distribution is like a smooth histogram and indicates how likely a random variable takes a value in a specific region.
c.
If the density of a population at a point x is smaller than the one of another population, then also the cumulative distribution function of the first population is smaller than the other one.

The cumulative distribution function is strictly increasing everywhere for all distributions. Therefore it can never quite reach 0 or 1.

Die richtige Antwort ist: The cumulative distribution at y is the density integrated up to y.
